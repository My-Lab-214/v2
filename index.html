Big Natue Water World

Oceans cover over 70% of Earth's surface. They absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, regulate climate, and produce more than half of the planet’s oxygen through phytoplankton photosynthesis. Marine habitats range from sunlit coral reefs and kelp forests to dark abyssal plains and hydrothermal vents. Oceans support diverse life, including fish, mammals, invertebrates, and microorganisms, many of which are vital for human food security and economies. Ocean currents distribute heat, influence weather patterns, and connect distant ecosystems. However, pollution, overfishing, acidification, and warming threaten marine biodiversity and ecological balance, highlighting the need for sustainable practices and marine conservation. They help control the weather and temperature by absorbing carbon dioxide and producing oxygen.
